Political Corruption in Spain

Spanish

Spain faces new elections this Sunday after parties failed to form a government. The general election held on 20 December resulted in no party having a majority. Neither could an alliance of the right between PP and Ciudadanos , nor the left between Socialist Workers (PSOE) and Podemos reach enough number of seats.

Dissatisfaction has been growing among a large, mainly young chunk of Spain that is fed up with the traditional way of doing politics, and the corruption scandals which have damaged the old parties.

The large number of scandals makes it difficult to keep track of the corruption cases that expand throughout the country. Spanish politics used to be predictable, the dominance of the PP and PSOE made things almost boring. However, the landscape has shifted and things are infinitely more unpredictable because citizens are voting for new parties and a prosper future for the country.

General elections will be held on June 26th. If you are a Spanish citizen, remember that the most effective way to fight corruption is in your right to vote. If you are not, help us by spreading the word :)
